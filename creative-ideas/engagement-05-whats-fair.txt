what does fair actually mean when it comes to freelancer pay structures?

because 0% commission sounds great, but how does a platform even survive on that? 20% is supposedly "industry standard" but is it really or did we just accept that because everyone else did? agencies take 40-60% and claim they're worth it.

so what would actually be fair to the person doing the work?

maybe it's 0% and the platform figures out a different business model. maybe 5-10% if it's transparent. maybe a flat fee per project. or something else entirely that we haven't thought of yet.

@Human Pages is betting the answer is 0%.

but i'm asking you: what feels fair?
