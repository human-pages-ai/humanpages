05
Addressing Concerns

But What About...

We anticipated the objections. Here's why each one is solvable.

🛑 "Smart contracts are a security liability. One bug and we lose user funds."
Valid concern. Three mitigations: 1) The contract is ~200 lines with no complex DeFi logic — just lock, release, dispute. It's simpler than a Uniswap pool. 2) We use OpenZeppelin's audited ReentrancyGuard and SafeERC20. 3) We deploy on Base first (low gas, fast iteration), limit escrow to $1,000 max per job during beta, and get a security review before removing the cap.
Beta safeguard: MAX_ESCROW = 1000 USDC. Upgradeable via admin multisig.
🛑 "This adds gas costs to every job. Our 0% fee promise is gone."
On Base L2, a USDC transfer costs ~$0.01. Escrow adds one extra transaction (lock + release = 2 txns instead of 1 direct transfer). Total extra cost: ~$0.01 per job. On a $500 job, that's 0.002%. We still honestly say "0% platform fees" because we're not charging — the chain charges fractions of a penny.
Base gas: ~$0.005/txn. Two txns = $0.01 total. Ethereum fallback: ~$2-5/txn.
🛑 "We said 'HumanPages never touches your money.' Escrow breaks that."
HumanPages still never touches the money. The smart contract does. It's non-custodial — we don't have a withdrawal key. The contract is immutable code that executes deterministically. We're not Coinbase holding your funds. We're a vending machine: put money in, get result out, no human in the middle. This is actually more aligned with our brand than the current trust-the-stranger model.
🛑 "Who's the arbitrator? We become judge and jury."
Three-tier resolution: 1) Auto-resolve — if agent doesn't dispute within 48h of delivery, funds auto-release. 95% of jobs end here. 2) Mutual resolution — agent and human agree on a split (e.g., 70/30). Contract executes it. 3) Arbitration — only for the ~2% that escalate. Initially us (admin multisig), later a DAO or Kleros-style decentralized court. We start simple.
🛑 "This delays our roadmap by weeks. We have other priorities."
Without escrow, every other feature we build has a ceiling. We can ship recurring jobs, file sharing, and bulk hiring — but users still won't trust the platform with real money. Escrow is the foundation. We build it in 2 weeks, and every feature after it compounds on a trusted base. Without it, we're decorating a house with no foundation.
Week 1: Contract + backend integration. Week 2: Frontend UI + deploy on Base.

08
Scope Control

What We're NOT Building

This proposal is intentionally minimal. Here's what's explicitly out of scope for the 2-week sprint.

✅ In Scope

One escrow contract, deployed on Base
USDC only (our primary token)
Lock, release, dispute — 3 operations
$1,000 max cap during beta
48h auto-release (no infinite holds)
Admin multisig for arbitration (us, initially)
Optional — agents choose escrow or direct pay

❌ Out of Scope (Later)

Multi-chain escrow (Ethereum, Polygon, Arbitrum)
USDT / DAI escrow support
Milestone-based partial releases
Decentralized arbitration (Kleros, UMA)
Streaming escrow (Superfluid + lock)
Cross-chain escrow bridges
Insurance fund / slashing
The principle: Ship the smallest escrow that solves the trust problem. Base chain, USDC only, $1K cap, admin arbitration. We can expand to multi-chain, multi-token, decentralized arbitration later — but the trust gap closes on Day 14.
